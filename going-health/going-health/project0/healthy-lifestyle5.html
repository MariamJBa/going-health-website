Swimming

Health and sports research indicates that swimming for half an hour a day lowers blood pressure, strengthens the heart, reduces cholesterol in the blood and increases the efficiency of blood circulation.

Swimming for an hour burns between 250 and 500 calories, depending on the strength and speed of the swim; Therefore, this sport is considered one of the best exercises for weight loss.

Water makes the body appear lighter than it really is, so swimming is one of the most suitable sports for people who are overweight.

Swimming keeps the heart and lungs in good shape.

Archery

Archery is the practice or skill of using a bow to throw arrows. It requires the skills of precision, control, focus, repetition and determination. They are available to be practiced by everyone, regardless of age, gender or ability.

The benefits of Archery are many. It works to strengthen the muscles of the body because it depends on stability and balance using the muscles of the trunk and the lower part of the body, indicating that it also works to increase the strength of the shoulders and arms through drag and resistance, arc and tendon, and raise the level of focus in terms of controlling the movements of the body and the art of hitting the target accurately.

Running

Running is a type of physical sport that moves the muscles and makes them in continuous movement, leading to strengthening them and increasing their mass. It is important in all cases and for all ages, from the young to the adult and the elderly. Running is also important to move blood circulation, as it is one of the reasons that prevent and reduce cardiovascular disease.

Here are some activities that we can do for a healthy life-style.

Walking

Walking is one of the easiest sports in the world, as it can be done at any time and anywhere without any cost.

There are different types of Walking. There is slow walking, which is what we usually do during the day, and fast walking which requires larger steps in the shortest possible time.

Tennis

It has been proven that regular tennis practice helps reduce body fat percentage. Tennis players can burn between 400 and 600 calories per hour.

Cardio: Regular training is a great way to improve your cardio and tennis. Work out your cardio regularly. When you run across the court to play the ball during the point you reach a high level of movement and then lower that level when you rest for 20-30 seconds between the two points.

Flexibility: Tennis forces the player to stretch dozens of muscles in all parts of the body, including muscles you don't know.
